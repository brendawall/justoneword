Offering a new perspective on the word...

My mind is like a 'magic porridge pot'. And as a biblical translator it overflows. Still, a painful process - as I need to be very critical of previous translations. All translations into English (from the Hebrew, Greek and Latin text) are necesarrily victim, ie influenced by the times and context from which they arise. There, put starkly, is the problem.
These Just One Words try to achieve two aims. The first is to set just one word is in its historical and theological context. But, how we see the context, changes rapidly as new manuscripts are discovered (eg, the Dead Sea Scrolls in the 1950s). The second aim of these words is to illuminate their current relevance - not just too religious thinking, but to the very way we view life in all its manifestations. For example, much blood has been shed and lives ended by the translation of the greek word for 'repent'. The wonder of this word is strangely an apparent reversal. Instead, it can mean 'being given a renewed mind'
At the beginning of St. Johns Gospel, he says 'In the beginning was the word'. I hope these words of mine carry on the embodiment that John new about. One of his key words is the word 'glory'. I hope these Just One Words are radiant for you.
